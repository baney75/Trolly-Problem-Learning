Philosophy Explored

Consequentialism

Actions judged by outcomes. The morally right action produces the best overall results. Utilitarianism maximizes total happiness and minimizes suffering for all affected.

Key Thinkers: Bentham, Mill, Singer

Deontology

Morality grounded in duties and rules, not consequences. Kant's Categorical Imperative: act only on principles that could become universal laws, treat persons as ends in themselves.

Key Thinkers: Kant, Ross, Korsgaard
